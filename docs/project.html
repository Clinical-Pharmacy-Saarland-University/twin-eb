The project

How TWIN-EB is built

TWIN-EB combines mechanistic modelling with a prospective clinical study to establish evidence-based, individualised losartan dosing for children with RDEB. The work is organised into five connected components over three years.

Components

The five components

1

Adult PBPK model of losartan

A whole-body physiologically based pharmacokinetic model of losartan, built with the Open Systems Pharmacology suite, as the mechanistic foundation.

2

RDEB virtual twin population

Scaling the model to children and incorporating RDEB-specific physiology, implemented in R with Open Systems Pharmacology.

3

Population PK/PD models

Establishing the dose–concentration–effect relationship using data from the REFLECT trial, the LESS-EB study and the literature, in NONMEM.

4

The LESS-EB clinical study

A prospective study measuring losartan and its metabolite together with disease biomarkers in children with RDEB; see below.

5

A decision-support tool

A prototype web application, in R/Shiny, so clinicians can simulate an individual child's dose.

Clinical study

The LESS-EB clinical study

LESS-EB (Losartan PharmacokinEticS and PharmacodynamicS in RDEB) is a prospective, open-label, multi-centre study in children and young people with molecularly confirmed moderate-to-severe RDEB, conducted at specialist EB centres in Germany and Greece and led by Prof. Dr. Dimitra Kiritsi. It is designed to keep the burden on participants low.

  • Sparse plasma sampling (typically 2–4 samples) for losartan and its active metabolite EXP3174
  • Biomarkers of inflammation, disease activity and growth
  • Optional untargeted metabolomics and ADME genotyping
  • Standard safety assessments, alongside usual care

Recruitment begins only once the study protocol has the required ethical and regulatory approvals.

Timeline

Timeline

Year 1 · 2026–2027

Foundations

Build the adult PBPK model, begin the RDEB virtual twins, and finalise the LESS-EB protocol and approvals.

Year 2 · 2027–2028

Twins and data

Complete the virtual twin population, recruit and sample participants, and develop the population PK/PD models.

Year 3 · 2028–2029

Validation and tool

Validate the models against the clinical data, build the decision-support prototype, and derive dosing recommendations.
