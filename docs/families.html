Information for families

The TWIN-EB project is built on the belief that new technologies can help improve treatment and outcomes for children living with RDEB. Guided by experienced doctors and specialist centres, the clinical study LESS-EB places families and patients at the heart of its work. The team is committed to listening to patients’ needs, experiences, and interests every step of the way.

About the study

LESS-EB is a clinical study for babies, children, and young people with recessive dystrophic epidermolysis bullosa, also known as RDEB. The study looks at treatment with losartan and aims to better understand how children with RDEB develop over time while receiving this medicine.

One important question is how the disease changes during treatment: for example, whether symptoms remain stable, improve, or continue to progress. The study also measures how the body absorbs, processes, and clears losartan. This helps doctors learn more about suitable dosing for children of different ages, using evidence rather than estimates.

Around 30 patients are expected to take part in the study, starting from newborns and infants through to children and young people. LESS-EB is led by Prof. Dr. Dimitra Kiritsi and carried out at specialist EB centres in Germany and Greece, where experienced teams care for children and families affected by EB.

How the project studies losartan

To understand how losartan may best be used in children with RDEB, the TWIN-EB project uses two types of computer-based modelling: PBPK modelling and population PK modelling.

PBPK modelling can be imagined as a “virtual body model.” It helps researchers understand how losartan moves through the body: how it is absorbed, distributed, broken down, and removed. The model can be adapted for children of different ages and sizes, and for features that may be important in RDEB.

Population PK modelling looks at information from a group of patients. It helps researchers see why losartan levels may differ from one child to another, even when children receive similar doses. For example, age, body weight, growth, disease activity, or other individual factors may influence how the body handles the medicine.

Together, these models help researchers learn from both clinical study data, including the earlier REFLECT trial and the LESS-EB study, and computer simulations. The goal is to support safer, more evidence-based dosing of losartan for children with RDEB, moving away from estimates and toward treatment decisions that better reflect each child’s needs.

Registering your interest

You can register your interest at any time, and we will keep you informed as the study opens. Registering is not a commitment and does not enrol a child in the study. Whether a child is eligible is decided together with the clinical team, in line with the approved protocol, and recruitment begins only once approvals are in place.

You can also write to twineb@uni-saarland.de. The project is coordinated at Clinical Pharmacy, Saarland University.
